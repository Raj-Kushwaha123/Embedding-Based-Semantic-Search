    Efficient Transformer-Based Architectures for Low-Resource NLP:
    A Comparative Study of Embedding Strategies
================================================================================

Authors: Priya Sharma¹, Aditya Mehta², Lakshmi Narayan¹
¹ Department of Computer Science, IIIT Guwahati, India
² AI Research Lab, NovaTech Solutions, Bangalore, India
Published: Proceedings of the International Conference on Computational
Linguistics and NLP (ICCL-NLP), March 2026


ABSTRACT
--------
The transformer architecture has become the de facto standard for natural
language processing (NLP) tasks, yet its application to low-resource languages
remains challenging due to limited training corpora and computational
constraints. In this paper, we present a comparative study of embedding
strategies — including static word embeddings, contextual subword embeddings,
and sentence-level dense representations — for downstream NLP tasks in Hindi,
Bengali, and Tamil. We introduce a lightweight distillation framework that
compresses multilingual transformer models into efficient student networks
with fewer than 25 million parameters, achieving within 3% of the teacher
model's accuracy on named entity recognition (NER), sentiment analysis, and
semantic textual similarity (STS) benchmarks. Our results demonstrate that
sentence-level embeddings generated by distilled models offer the best
trade-off between computational cost and task performance for retrieval-
augmented generation (RAG) pipelines in low-resource settings.


1. INTRODUCTION
---------------
The advent of transformer-based language models such as BERT (Devlin et al.,
2019), GPT (Radford et al., 2018), and T5 (Raffel et al., 2020) has
fundamentally reshaped the landscape of NLP. These models leverage self-
attention mechanisms to capture long-range dependencies in text, enabling
state-of-the-art performance across a wide range of benchmarks including
GLUE, SuperGLUE, and SQuAD. However, the majority of research and pre-
training data is concentrated in English and a handful of high-resource
languages, leaving a significant performance gap for the 100+ languages
spoken across the Indian subcontinent.

Recent multilingual models such as mBERT, XLM-RoBERTa, and IndicBERT have
made strides in bridging this gap, yet their large parameter counts (often
exceeding 270 million) make deployment on edge devices or in latency-
sensitive applications impractical. This motivates our investigation into
efficient embedding strategies that preserve semantic fidelity while
drastically reducing model size.


2. METHODOLOGY
--------------
We evaluate three categories of embedding approaches:

2.1 Static Word Embeddings
    We train FastText skip-gram models on curated monolingual corpora for
    each target language (approximately 500 MB of cleaned text per language).
    Embedding dimension is fixed at 300. These vectors serve as our baseline.

2.2 Contextual Subword Embeddings
    We fine-tune XLM-RoBERTa-base on language-specific masked language
    modeling (MLM) objectives for 50,000 additional steps using a learning
    rate of 2e-5 and batch size 64. Token-level representations from the
    last four hidden layers are mean-pooled to produce 768-dimensional
    contextual embeddings.

2.3 Sentence-Level Dense Embeddings
    We apply knowledge distillation to train a compact sentence encoder
    based on the all-MiniLM-L6-v2 architecture (Wang et al., 2020). The
    student model produces 384-dimensional sentence embeddings and is
    trained with a combined loss of cosine similarity and cross-entropy
    on 1.2 million translated sentence pairs sourced from OPUS and
    Samanantar parallel corpora.


3. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
--------------------------
Evaluation is performed on three downstream tasks:

  Task                         | Static | Contextual | Sentence (Ours)
  -----------------------------|--------|------------|----------------
  NER (F1 Score)               |  71.2  |    88.4    |     85.9
  Sentiment Analysis (Acc.)    |  76.8  |    91.3    |     89.1
  Semantic Similarity (Spear.) |  62.4  |    84.7    |     82.3
  Retrieval Recall@10          |  44.1  |    79.6    |     77.8
  Inference Latency (ms/query) | 0.8    |   42.3     |      4.1

Key observations:
  • Contextual embeddings achieve the highest accuracy across all tasks but
    incur ~10× higher inference latency compared to our distilled model.
  • Our sentence-level embeddings trail contextual embeddings by only 2–3
    percentage points while being an order of magnitude faster at inference.
  • Static embeddings remain useful for vocabulary-level tasks but fail to
    capture polysemy and contextual nuance, leading to poor retrieval recall.
  • For RAG pipelines where both retrieval quality and latency matter, the
    distilled sentence encoder provides the optimal balance.


4. CONCLUSION
-------------
We have demonstrated that distilled sentence-level embeddings offer a
compelling alternative to full-scale contextual models for low-resource
Indic NLP tasks. The all-MiniLM-L6-v2 distillation framework successfully
compresses semantic understanding into a 22-million-parameter model that
runs efficiently on CPU hardware, making it suitable for deployment in
resource-constrained environments such as local RAG pipelines, mobile
applications, and edge inference servers. Future work will extend this
approach to additional Indic languages and explore quantization techniques
for further size reduction.
